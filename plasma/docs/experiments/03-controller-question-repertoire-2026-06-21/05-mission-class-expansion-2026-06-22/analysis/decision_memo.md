# Decision Memo

## Decision

The adaptive controller hypothesis is strengthened, but not settled enough for
a universal product default.

This clean expansion should be treated as evidence that controller question
strategy materially changes research/report quality beyond the M5 code-analysis
pilot. The strongest next product/research step is a smaller V2-versus-V3
follow-up with a repertoire-selection rule, not a direct promotion of V3.

## Evidence

| Mission | Class | Blind winner | Variant | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| M1 | narrow-source | K1 | V2 | A one-time creative switch plus recovery beat baseline, detect-only, and always-divergent. |
| M3 | broad-topic | K2 | V3 | Scheduled divergence produced the broadest and most actionable product-flow report. |
| M6 | source-conflict | K4 | V3 | Scheduled divergence best preserved conflict boundaries and produced concrete default/legacy/experiment separation. |

The earlier contaminated attempt was discarded. In the clean run, every resumed
turn launched from the mission source directory. Conservative flags remained in
some manifests, but manual review found them to be filter false positives rather
than material source-corpus contamination.

## Interpretation

The result rejects the weakest version of the baseline hypothesis: "ordinary
continuation/narrowing is enough." In this slice, V0 did not win any mission.

The result also does not prove that "always divergent" should become the
product default. V3 won M3 and M6, but V2 won M1. That split matters because the
Kirov plan explicitly says mission-class splits should lead to a
repertoire-selection experiment rather than one default.

The promising shape is:

- Use continuation/narrowing while the agent is still reading useful source
  material.
- When the answer starts repeating or staying too abstract, use a creative
  switch question.
- After a switch, recover direction by asking what changed, what remains
  unresolved, what can be dropped, and what source/read action matters next.
- For broad product-flow or source-conflict work, scheduled divergence may be
  helpful, but its cost and transcript readability need transcript-quality
  judging before productization.

## Product Implication

Keep the C1 product boundary intact:

- The controller remains question-only.
- It does not create evidence, claims, confidence, source candidates, saved
  knowledge, report body, or citations.
- It steers the same main agent session through user-style questions.
- Source access remains MCP/source-read based; do not compensate by stuffing
  source packs into prompts.

The next product experiment should add controller observability and a small
selection layer, not revive the legacy ledger machinery.

## Recommended Next Experiment

Run a smaller follow-up:

- Variants: V2 and V3 only, plus the current no-controller C1 baseline if cost
  permits.
- Missions: at least one narrow-source, one broad-topic, one source-conflict,
  and one code-analysis replay.
- Judge views: final-report-only and transcript-quality.
- Primary question: when should Plasma choose one-time creative switch versus
  scheduled divergence?
- Stop condition: if V3 improves final reports but worsens transcript
  readability or source discipline, prefer V2 plus explicit stagnation signals.

## Current Decision

Do not implement a universal adaptive default yet.

Proceed toward a controller-led C1 experiment path where the controller can
select from a small question repertoire and where every selection is logged for
debugging. Treat V2 as the safer product candidate and V3 as a stronger but
costlier candidate that needs transcript-quality validation.
